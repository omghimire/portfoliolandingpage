Carbon Cycle Imbalance (‡§ï‡§æ‡§∞‡•ç‡§¨‡§® ‡§ö‡§ï‡•ç‡§∞‡§ï‡•ã ‡§Ö‡§∏‡§®‡•ç‡§§‡•Å‡§≤‡§®)

The carbon cycle is a fundamental natural process that maintains Earth‚Äôs temperature and supports life. It involves the continuous exchange of carbon between the atmosphere, oceans, land, and living organisms. Historically, this cycle remained in a delicate equilibrium where the carbon released by natural sources was balanced by "sinks" like forests and oceans.However, since the Industrial Revolution, human activities have caused a significant imbalance. The primary driver is the burning of fossil fuels, which releases carbon that was stored underground for millions of years into the atmosphere in a very short time. Additionally, widespread deforestation has reduced the Earth's capacity to absorb carbondioxide . Excess atmospheric carbon traps more heat, resulting in global warming and unpredictable climate change. Furthermore, oceans are becoming more acidic as they absorb surplus carbondioxide, threatening marine life. Restoring this balance requires a dual approach: drastically reducing carbon emissions through renewable energy and protecting natural carbon sinks through reforestation. Addressing this imbalance is no longer an option but a necessity for the survival of our ecosystems.
